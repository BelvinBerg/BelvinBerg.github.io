Hazlewood State Waiver Benefits

Texas offers the Hazlewood exemption program for veterans and eligible dependents. This state benefit can supplement or provide an alternative to federal benefits. Review the options below to find the program that matches your situation.

Hazlewood - Standard

For: Veterans utilizing their own benefit (up to 150 hours)

Documents & Requirements:

  • DD214
  • Hazlewood Exemption Application
  • Texas residency proof
  • Certificate of eligibility showing no federal educational benefits available
  • Meet SHSU's Financial Aid GPA requirements

Hazlewood - Legacy

For: Veterans sharing 150 hours benefit with eligible dependents (spouse, biological child, stepchild, adopted child, or tax dependent)

Dependent Eligibility:

  • Must be younger than 25 years old
  • Texas residency required for both student and veteran

Documents & Requirements:

  • DD214
  • Hazlewood Exemption Application
  • Certificate of eligibility showing no federal educational benefits available
  • Proof of relationship (birth certificate, marriage license, adoption documents, tax transcripts)
  • Meet SHSU's Financial Aid GPA requirements
  • Selective Service registration for male dependents

Hazlewood - Non-Legacy (Disability)

For: All eligible dependents of veterans who are totally and permanently disabled, meet individual unemployability requirements, are Missing in Action (MIA), or Killed in Action (KIA) — with 150 hours benefit

Veteran Eligibility:

  • 100% Permanent and Total VA disability rating, OR
  • Individual unemployability rating from VA, OR
  • Missing in Action (MIA), OR
  • Killed in Action (KIA)

Documents & Requirements:

  • DD214
  • Hazlewood Exemption Application
  • Certificate of eligibility showing no GI Bill® educational benefits available
  • Proof of relationship (birth certificate, marriage license, adoption documents, tax transcripts)
  • Student must be Texas resident (veteran residency not required)
  • No defaulted Texas guaranteed loans
  • Meet SHSU's Financial Aid GPA requirements
  • Selective Service registration for male dependents
Please Note: Hazlewood does not pay for housing, meals, or book stipends. Additionally, this waiver may not fully cover your tuition and fees.

Active Duty & Guard/Reserve Support

Using Tuition Assistance?
If you wish to use Tuition Assistance, you must speak with your Educational Services Officer (ESO) or counselor at your base first to begin that process. If you are a prospective student, you must receive approval from your ESO, military counselor, or Service prior to enrollment.

Federal Benefits

Veterans and military-connected students can access multiple federal GI Bill programs offering tuition coverage, monthly stipends, and housing allowances.

Learn which federal benefit is right for you by reviewing the comparison table above and your VA Certificate of Eligibility.

State Benefits (Texas)

The Hazlewood Exemption provides tuition waivers for eligible Texas veterans and their dependents as an alternative or supplement to federal benefits.

No housing, meal, or book stipends provided — verify with Financial Aid that benefits cover your costs.

Need Help?

If you have questions about which benefit to pursue, need help gathering documentation, or want to discuss your specific situation, our Veterans Benefits Processing Team is here to help.

Contact the team through the chatbot or visit our office for personalized guidance.

Getting Started

  1. Determine Your Eligibility: Review the benefit summaries above to identify which program(s) you may qualify for.
  2. Gather Your Documents: Collect required paperwork (DD214, residency proof, etc.) listed under each benefit type.
  3. Submit Your Application: Apply through VA (federal benefits) or SHSU Financial Aid (Hazlewood state benefits).
  4. Follow Up: Check application status and reach out if you have questions during processing.
